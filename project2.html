Feature #1

I transformed our research and ideas into an interactive prototype. The first feature is a quiz that assesses users' work and personal interests, helping them navigate the overwhelming number of majors (The questions listed are examples that can be expanded upon for further detail). Based on their responses, the quiz generates a personalized list of major recommendations.

Feature #2

This feature allows users to explore their list of recommended majors, with the ability to filter by "highly," "moderately," and "least." As users explore each major, it is automatically crossed off the list, and the number of explored majors increases. Additionally, users can write journal entries to track insights and reflections throughout their journey.

Feature #3

The final feature provides a list of majors available at the user's college, allowing them to dive deeper into each major's details. It includes information on campus resources such as internships, student organizations, and key contacts, offering students opportunities to connect with individuals who can share more about their experiences. This helps undecided students gain a clearer understanding of potential career paths and what to expect moving forward.

Refining the user experience

Through usability testing, we identified key areas for improvement and made targeted changes to enhance navigation, organization, and overall user flow.

Revision #1

From the four usability tests, I gathered that users appreciated the checklist feature but expressed interest in seeing a progress count. Based on this feedback, I added a counter displaying the number of majors explored out of the total, helping users better understand their progress as they navigate through the options.

2. What if I make the “wrong” choice?

Originally, under each major, students could explore details like the major description, career paths, campus clubs, networking opportunities, and job prospects. However, during user testing, I observed that the process of clicking in and out of each category was tedious. To improve usability, I consolidated these categories into one section and added a toggle feature, allowing users to easily switch between different pieces of information without navigating away from the page.

What I’d Do Next Time

What I learned...

The main takeaway from this project is the importance of being on the same page and having a clear plan before executing. Without this, you risk spending extra time revisiting and refining your work to get it right.

1. Power of having a vision

A key lesson I learned was the importance of having a clear vision before diving into design. While prototyping, I focused on simplicity and functionality, but the result lacked visual appeal. This led me to invest more time in redesigning the interface to be both aesthetically pleasing and user-friendly.

2. Clear communication

After our brainstorming presentation left the audience confused, we recognized the need for better alignment within the team. We gathered, shared our ideas, and combined the strengths of each concept to create a unified and clearer vision for our prototype.

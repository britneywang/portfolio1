Campus Connect

Class Project — a platform for
undecided college students to find
their major

For my Shark Tank Simulation (CAS251) class at Penn State University last semester, we were tasked with addressing a societal challenge. I shared my personal experience of navigating the complex process of selecting a major, which resonated with two colleagues who joined the project. We adopted the lean startup methodology and I applied design thinking to guide our process. The goal and my primary intention was to develop an app that would help undecided students—like I had been—easily navigate their career paths.

Problem

How can we simplify the process for undecided college students to choose their major more effectively?

Result

I designed and developed a high-fidelity app that streamlines the process.

I found this project particularly rewarding as it demonstrated how the principles of design thinking extend beyond my UX classes. Week 4-6 — User Research

Understanding users
with interviews & affinity mapping

We set out to understand the experiences of undergraduate college students selecting their majors across all academic years and career paths. We conducted a semi-structured interview with a focus group of 20+ students, followed by 12 individual interviews where I led 4 of one-on-one sessions. As I organized the data using affinity mapping in Miro, clear patterns emerged, revealing key insights into students' pains and struggles. Here's what we learned.

There are too many majors to choose from, where do I start?

With so many possible career paths, it’s hard to know where to start or which one will truly align with your interests and goals. This uncertainty often leaves students feeling unsure of how to match their interests with a career.

What if I make the “wrong” choice?

Many students hesitate when choosing a major because they fear it won't align with their true interests or future goals. This uncertainty creates an illusion that one "wrong" decision could set them off course in their college careers, causing them to delay or avoid the decision

Is this what I want to pursue or what I’m expected to do?

External pressures—like family expectations—often steer students toward majors that don’t truly match their passions, leading to further misalignment between what they want and what they feel they "should" pursue.

Bringing insights to life with wireframes

Feature #1

In our brainstorming phase, we focused on tackling the major decision process head-on. We knew students needed a way to connect their interests with potential careers. Our first wireframe addresses this with a quiz feature, guiding users through a series of questions to filter majors based on their unique interests, passions, and strengths.

Feature #2

Once we had the right majors filtered, we wanted students to understand what each path truly offers. Our second wireframe introduces a major profile for each filtered result, showcasing key details like job descriptions, networking opportunities, and additional resources. This feature empowers students with a comprehensive view of what their career could look like.

Feature #3

The decision-making process can feel daunting, especially with external pressures. To help students reflect on their choices and align them with their personal goals, our third wireframe introduces a journal feature. This allows students to track their thoughts, concerns, and motivations as they explore their options, bringing them closer to a decision that feels right for them.

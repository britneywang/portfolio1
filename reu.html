What did I learn over the 10 week research experience?

Over this past summer, I worked as an UX researcher at the University of Michigan-Flint for the National Science Foundation REU. I joined a team of 3 other student researchers and our mentor. We focused on a human centered approach in understanding how consent is exchanged between people with aurally and visually impairments in online to in person dating environments.

During the course of 10 weeks alongside my team, I applied for the IRB, digested literature reviews, created an 80 question Qualtrics survey flow, and analyzed 50+ responses with reflective thematic analysis.

Study Plan

Our goal


The goal for the second week was to create a plan to recruit at least 20 participants, finalize a process for our study, and submit the IRB. One thing I learned about research is that it’s important to be flexible and act accordingly.


What we actually accomplished:

For the most part we accomplished our tasks…

— Defining our recruitment criteria (e.g. having visually or aurally impariment)
— Determining our research platforms to recruit (Prolific) and conduct our study (qualtrics)
— Completed the PEERRS Certification for understanding human subjects research and responsible conduct (part of the IRB requirement)
— Finalized our method to run a survey — we considered running an in-person workshop or hybrid study but because of the specific criterias, we concluded it might be difficult to meet all the participants in person geographically, financially, comfortability, especially in the short time frame we working in



What happened to the IRB

well, we didn’t end up getting approval until week seven. Between that time frame of modifying our application, we revised our survey structure and brainstormed to continue making it the way we wanted to.

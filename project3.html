The University of
Michigan-Flint

Last summer, I spent 10 weeks at the University of Michigan-Flint conducting UX research. Collaborating with 3 researchers and a mentor, I immersed myself in literature reviews, developed a comprehensive study plan, conducted a survey, and synthesized the findings by creating key themes. As this was my first experience in academia, it was a steep learning curve, but working hands-on with such a supportive team was incredibly rewarding.

Research Question

How is computer-mediated consent exchanged among aurally and visually disabled populations?

Result

With my team, I created an 80+ question survey receiving insights from 200 participants.

Survey Construction

More Literature Reviews to Inform Survey

Receiving multiple rejections from the IRB, I reviewed additional literature to further refine the survey structure and resolve any inconsistencies. After a couple weeks later, the survey was finally approved.




Analysis Process

Made Survey Revisions as Needed

With a goal of reaching 200 participants, the survey was released to 20 participants to ensure the data being given was what we were seeking. Along with the team, I made revisions with the survey questions when needed.
